IMPORTANT UPDATES

    SB23-169: Effective 08/07/2023, Previous law allowed a person 18 tyears of age or older possess or purchase a firearm (shotgun or longun). This law increased age to legally purchase a firearm to 21 years of age or older. Exceptions included active members of US armed forces or peace office/ certified P.O.S.T. board. See https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/sb23-169 for additional details.
    HB24-1348: Starting 01/01/2025, Secure firearm storage in vehicle. Handguns in an unattended vehicle must be stored in a locked hard-sided container that is placed out of plain view and the container is in a locked vehicle. See https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/hb24-1348 for additional details.
    HB24-1349: Starting 04/01/2025, New 6.5% excise tax on the net taxable sales of firearm dealers, firearms manufacturers, and ammunition vendors (vendors) from the retail sale of any firearm, firearm precursor part, or ammunition in Colorado. See https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/hb24-1349 for additional details.
    HB24-1353: Starting 07/01/2025, Firearm dealer permit (state permit) required in order to engage in the business of dealing in firearms other than destructive devices in Colorado. See https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/hb24-1353 for additional details.
    HB24-1174: Starting 07/01/2025, the requirements for obtaining a concealed carry permit will change mandating mandates that training classes to qualify for a concealed carry permit offer at least eight hours of instruction, including a live-fire test where participants shoot at least 50 rounds. Passing the live-fire test, as well as a written exam, will also be mandatory to get a concealed carry permit. See https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/hb24-1174 for additional details.
